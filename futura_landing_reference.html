Landing page — element by element
Every element numbered in order from top to bottom. Hand this file + the brief to Claude Code.
1 Slogan
"Your future, written in the palm of your hand."
Under the logo. Small, italic, gold. Double meaning lands immediately — palm as body part and palm as control.
2 Orb — upgraded
Two counter-rotating arc rings. Faint palm emoji inside pulsing. Radial gold glow behind. The orb must feel alive — like it is actively reading something right now.
3 Eyebrow
"5,000 years of wisdom · AI precision"
Credibility before the headline. Ancient + modern = not a gimmick. Sets up the claim before it's made.
4 Headline
"The one question every human cannot stop asking — answered."
The universal hook. Nobody is immune to curiosity about their future. "Answered" is the most powerful word on the page — it promises resolution to a lifelong question.
5 Subline
"5,000 years of palmistry. The precision of AI. Your future is not a mystery. It's already written — in the lines of your hand."
Three jobs: credibility (ancient), technology (AI), reframe (not mystery — already there). "Your future is not a mystery" in bold breaks the user's assumption that the future is unknowable.
6 Curiosity quote box
"What is going to happen to me?" — The only question every human shares. Now — finally — answered.
"Finally" is the most loaded word in this box. It implies years of frustration and the sudden relief of having an answer. Mirrors the exact thought already in the user's head.
7 Feature cards
4 cards. Palm (the hook), Chat with Live badge (converts curious to engaged), Daily updates with New Daily badge (creates habit loop FOMO), Key events with Subscribers badge (the subscription pitch without a paywall screen — seeing what subscribers get is the conversion trigger).
8 Proof strip
5K Yrs proven · 1 Palm. Yours. · ∞ Questions
"1 Palm. Yours." is the most important cell. In three words it says: this is not generic, this is not for everyone, this is specifically for you. That word "yours" does more conversion work than any other element on the page.
9 Blurred preview card
Show ~25 words of a real reading clearly. Blur the rest. Cut mid-sentence at "72 hours will..." — the incomplete sentence is physically uncomfortable to look away from. "Scan your palm to reveal the rest" is the micro-CTA that closes the deal before the main button.
10 CTA button
"Read my future — free"
"My" makes it personal. "Free" removes the last barrier. The dash creates a beat. Deeper gold with glow shadow — premium, not sandy. This button has to feel like something important is about to happen.
11 Trust line
"90 seconds · No account · Private · Free to start"
Four objection-killers in one line. "Private" is the new one — people are nervous about uploading a photo of their hand. Address it before they think to ask.
